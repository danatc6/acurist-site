The Problem
"9 out of 10 physicians who screen for cognitive decline use a tool that cannot reliably detect MCI."

The MMSE — still the most widely used cognitive screen in America — detects MCI with just 71% sensitivity and 36% specificity. That means for every 100 patients with early cognitive impairment, 29 are missed entirely, and 64 normal patients are falsely flagged. The MCI Screen was built specifically to solve this.
