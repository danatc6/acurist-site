Education

Why Early Action Matters

Understanding cognitive health means knowing where you stand — and acting while you have the most options.

Normal memory vs. mild impairment vs. decline

Memory changes with age. Some changes are normal, some indicate mild cognitive impairment, and some signal decline. Knowing which is which starts with a validated assessment — not guesswork.

The importance of early detection

Catching changes early — long before symptoms interfere with daily life — gives you the widest set of options. It’s also when interventions have the greatest chance of making a difference.

Prevention is possible through action

Many Alzheimer’s risk factors are modifiable. Sleep, vascular health, physical activity, and social engagement all play a role. Risk factors can be addressed early — and the earlier, the better.

What the Risk Profile does — and doesn’t do

The Alzheimer’s Risk Profile does not diagnose Alzheimer’s disease. It identifies your personal risk factors and provides a prevention strategy built on peer-reviewed science. Only a qualified clinician can make a diagnosis. Acurist is designed to inform and empower your conversations with your doctor.

FAQs

Your questions, answered.

Is this a diagnosis?
No. Our Memory Screen, MCI Screen, and Alzheimer’s Risk Profile are screening and risk-assessment tools — not diagnostic tests. They help decide whether further evaluation is needed. Only a qualified clinician can make a diagnosis.
How long does each assessment take?
Memory Screen: about 10 minutes. MCI Screen: about 15 minutes. Alzheimer’s Risk Profile: about 20–30 minutes. All are completed online at your own pace.
Does family history mean I’ll get Alzheimer’s?
Not necessarily. Family history increases risk but doesn’t determine outcome. Many modifiable factors — sleep, vascular health, physical activity, social engagement — influence risk. The Risk Profile translates your specific history into a prioritized action plan.
Will my doctor see this?
Only if you choose to share it. Every assessment produces a clinician-ready summary you can download as a PDF or send via FHIR export to your primary care physician or specialist.
What if I’m worried about cost?
Start with the Memory Screen at $29 for a quick baseline. You can upgrade to the MCI Screen or the full Alzheimer’s Risk Profile later if you want deeper insight. All options are HSA/FSA eligible.
What if my results are not normal?
Early detection opens up the most options. Many underlying causes of memory changes are reversible with proper intervention. Your report includes a clear next-step plan to share with your doctor.
