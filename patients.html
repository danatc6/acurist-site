FAQs

Your questions, answered.

What’s the difference between the Memory Screen and the MCI Screen?
The Memory Screen is our free at-home word memorization test — it gives you a quick baseline: is my memory normal for my age? The MCI Screen is the full clinical assessment: 97.3% accurate at detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment vs. normal aging, validated in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences. The $49 Alzheimer’s Risk Profile includes the full clinical MCI Screen.
What if I have a family history of Alzheimer’s Disease?
Individuals with a family history worry about what this means for them. The temptation may be to avoid a cognitive assessment. Don’t. You need to know as early as possible. Early detection provides the most options for treatment and informed decision-making.
What if I’m not yet 65 but have memory concerns?
10% of Americans aged 45 and older experience subjective cognitive decline — they notice changes in their ability to remember or think clearly. The Memory Screen and MCI Screen are designed for adults of all ages who want to understand their cognitive health baseline.
What if my results are not normal?
Early detection provides the most options for treatment and informed decision-making. Many underlying causes of MCI are reversible with proper intervention. Your report includes a clear next-step plan to share with your doctor.
